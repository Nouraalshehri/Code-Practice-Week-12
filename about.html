Hello, I am Omaima Kindasa, a Saudi fashion designer and entrepreneur

First of all, I am a craftswoman who has won a number of awards from the Chamber of Commerce, King Abdulaziz University and others in craft work. As for the fashion industry, it is a talent that I inherited from my mother, may God have mercy on her, who was a lover of heritage and skilled in making traditional costumes for us. What I like about this job. I kept all the old heritage pieces, renovated and restored them, and I always sewed clothes for my children, and designed for the daughters of the family. On the night of “Ghamra / Henna” for my daughter, I wore her five pieces of traditional costumes that I made with my own hands, and she won the approval of all the attendees. Praise be to God, I founded my brand “Contemporary Heritage”, which combines authenticity with modernity.

I am on a mission to revive the Saudi heritage

  • The skills need in traditional fashion

    Traditional costumes are handmade, and in order to master them, you must love them, be patient, and work with skill and mastery. My relationship with traditional costumes is an endless love. I research and study carefully, learning about the history and culture of the city and the region; To come out the dress is complete elements. He continued to work for 12 hours straight without getting bored, and he continued to work on one piece for 3 months. Because I am open to criticism, I develop the model once, two or three, and may even add a color or more; To come out satisfactorily and suitable for people's tastes.

  • Diversity and its impact on traditional fashion

    The Arabian Peninsula is a vast and sprawling subcontinent rich in diverse cultural heritage, of which traditional costumes are one of its vocabulary, and it varies in its diversity. And every region in the Kingdom has its own distinctive features. There is the Hijazi, Bedouin and coastal environment; Each of them is distinguished in its designs, lines, decorations and colors according to the surrounding environment, customs and traditions; The decorations and drawings on the clothes reflect the lifestyle and environment in which the person lives.
