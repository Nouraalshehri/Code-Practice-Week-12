Head hat Saudi southern costume

  • 17 Sep, 2020
  • Article

Women who work in agriculture in Asir may wear large hats made of wicker dom, known as the Htuffa, or the Tafsha, on their heads. It is a circular piece, with a diameter ranging between half a meter and a meter, with a spherical protrusion resembling the shape of a head, and it is made of fronds, or wicker by hand by inserting wicker pieces between each other until the Tafsha is formed in its final shape. The Tafsha is one of the old Asiri clothes that are placed over the head to protect against the sun’s heat, and it is widely used in Saudi Arabia and the Arabian Gulf, and even globally, but in different forms, and the goal is one.
